ABOUT US

University’s founders, E. Everett Cortright, a local physics and math teacher, Dr. Alfred Fones a local dentist and Sumner Simpson, President of Raybestos Corporation saw the need for an institution that provided a blend of traditional academia and hands on training to support the growing business, engineering and health care needs of the Bridgeport community.

They established the first junior college in Connecticut in 1927 to realize their vision and it lives on today at the University of Bridgeport. The newly established University of Bridgeport Student Consulting Practice is grounded in the founders’ vision with focused programs and services from the Ernest C. Trefz School of Business, the School of Engineering and the School of Health Sciences.!

With its internationally diverse student population and the University’s mission to provide students with the tools to launch their careers, our Student Consulting practice is ready to tackle your opportunities and provide the analysis, insights and processes to help accomplish your goals. Whether they be business, community or product based.

Today’s opportunities must serve a rapidly diversifying marketplace. What makes UB’s Student Consulting Practice unique is the diversity of its student body. We are eager to help!

MISSION & GOALS

MISSION

The Mission of the Consulting Practice is embodied in its logo and tagline line; “Bringing the Global View, Local.” Combining formal, disciplined University and Post Graduate training with unique hands-on experience, a diversified group of student consultants brings a perspective unlike any other.
The Global View is grounded in their life experience as foreign students in higher education in the US. Most have worked in their home countries prior to landing at UB and that work experience will serve both our Mission and our clients looking to improve all aspects of their enterprise.

GOALS

This Mission comes alive in the program’s Goals:
 Provide clients with best-in-class consulting across three areas of focus: Business, Engineering and Health Services
 Provide students with a dynamic, hands-on experience that not only complements their formal education, but starts their career with a competitive advantage when seeking to compete for opportunities.
 Deliver to the students and UB a revenue stream that can be utilized to fund the aspirations of the student practitioners and support for the University’s overall mission, preparing students for productive and fulfilling careers.

The Vision: A Practice to Both Learn and Serve

The Vision for the University of Bridgeport Student Consulting Practice is to evolve from a program that supports local businesses and
non-profits with much needed world class expertise in its chosen areas, business, engineering and health sciences.
In doing so, the student consultants will advance their career preparation.

As both the program’s reputation and experience grow, the client base will also mature. While continuing to serve the
greater Bridgeport area and the broader geography of Connecticut, muti-national companies will come to realize the benefits of the University’s
diverse student perspectives, skill sets and disciplines to provide the University and the program with an enhanced reputation.

INDIVIDUAL SCHOOL OVERVIEWS

Supported by local entrepreneur Ernest C. Trefz and his family, the School of Business at the University is at the cutting edge of some of businesses’ most in demand skill sets.

A leader in both AI Development and Data Analytics, student consultants will deliver AI-Powered business solutions using predictive analytics, customer segmentation and workflow automation. Complementing this focus on real business drivers, student consultants will provide insight into using AI and research driven insights into the areas of training and development, consumer and trade marketing, financial management and modeling, inventory optimization and predictive maintenance.

The University of Bridgeport’s School of Engineering’s faculty is world renowned for its work in Advanced Technology and Innovation, Aerospace and Mechanical Innovations and Sustainable Innovation and Green Technologies.

A multi-disciplinary faculty team will lead and diverse group of student consultants with expertise from aerospace engineering to sustainable design and AI/ML. The school’s robust academic research in these areas will deliver real world solutions to a broad array of business opportunities and issues as well as support for other academic institutions whose projects require additional support.

Delivering state of the art degree programs across a broad range of health science and care, student consultants and faculty advisors can provide local practitioners and academic institutions with support in both modern and traditional services in nursing, dental, nutrition and acupuncture and Chinese Herbal Medicine.

Consultants can also partner with both the School of Business and the School of Engineering to deliver an integrated approach to both the development of expertise in these areas of study but more effective and efficient processes for enhancing institutions and business opportunities.
